Idioms

Welcome to the Mandarin Idioms course! This course is for you if you have basic understanding in Chinese and you are interested in learning idioms (成语). Idioms are integral to Chinese culture and communication, each usually consisting of four characters that summarize stories and myths that the people live by.

In this course you will elevate your Chinese knowledge and speaking skills, while also gaining exposure to idioms the Chinese regularly use in their daily conversations!

Examples:

“守株待兔“


“守株待兔“ directly translates to “To Stand by a Tree Stump Waiting for a Hare”. One of the most famous and loved Chinese idiom stories. The idiom tells the story of a farmer who rises early in the morning everyday to work in his field. One day, he suddenly saw a hare running toward and hit its head on a tree. He said happily: “Great! I will have a delicious dinner.” From that day on, he sits under the tree waiting for hares to keep hitting on the tree. But the field had slowly become a mess and all his crops had died after he spent his days lazing away at the tree. No hares ever came by and hit the tree. Every Chinese kid is introduced to this story at a young age to motivate them to work hard to gain what they want.

Usage Examples:
平时学习不肯用功，考试想取得好成绩，这不是守株待兔吗?

You don't put in the effort to study but you want good grades. Isn't that like watching a tree stump for rabbits?
